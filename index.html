Welcome to my user manual

My basics

I'm years old. I'm married, born in Norway but live in Wigan. I have lived here for years. I'm half british. I have one month old son.

My simple rules

  • Don't be a dick
  • Don't ask to ask
  • A work place should be a fun place to work
  • Relax, it's going to be fine

How I work

My work/career is just part of me, I don't have a strict work/life separation. This means that sometimes I might be mowing the grass during work hours, and sometimes I will write some code on a quiet night on the couch. I have core hours between 9am and 2pm where I will generally be within 10 minutes of my desk. Outside these hours I might be painting a fence, trimming the hedges or playing with my son.

How I communicate

I always communicate directly, and rarely bother with the pleasantries (it's a cultural thing). I'm happy to have some small talk, but it should be after any business has been dealt with. It's always easiest to reach me in written form, preferably using async communication. I prefer communication in the following order:

  1. Slack
  2. Whatsapp/Text
  3. Email
  4. Homing pigeon
  5. Phone call
Note that email might take longer to get a reply, I don't check my email more than 2-3 times a day, and don't have any notifications turned on.

How to get on my good side

If you have something you need help with, always give me as much information as possible. I can decide what is relevant to me once I have the complete picture. Always try to include as much context as possible too.

What annoys me

  • People coming to me with problems without having tried a solution first. I'm always happy to help, but I would like to know what hasn't worked first.
  • People who take things too seriously. I live by the lyrics of "always look on the bright side of life". If you can't laugh at it, what can you do?
  • Unnecessary meetings that should have been an email
